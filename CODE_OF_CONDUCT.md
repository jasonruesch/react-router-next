# Code of Conduct

`react-router-next` is built by and for people who enjoy making good software together. This document describes the kind of community we want to be.

## Our shared values

We're a community of contributors and maintainers who care about the craft of building useful tools. We come from different backgrounds, experience levels, and corners of the world, and the project is better for it.

## Behaviors we encourage

- **Be welcoming.** Greet newcomers warmly. Remember that everyone was new once — including you. A friendly first interaction is often the difference between a one-time visitor and a long-term contributor.

- **Be patient and generous.** Assume the person on the other end is doing their best with the information they have. Give people time to respond, learn, or change their mind. When you have context someone else lacks, share it kindly.

- **Be curious.** Ask "what are you trying to do?" before "you're doing it wrong." Many of the best contributions start as questions.

- **Be specific and constructive.** When reviewing code, suggest concrete improvements. When reporting bugs, provide reproductions. When proposing features, describe the use case. Specifics make collaboration faster and friendlier.

- **Credit others generously.** Acknowledge prior work, link to inspirations, thank reviewers, and celebrate contributions from people newer than you. Recognition costs nothing and builds trust.

- **Disagree gracefully.** Reasonable people will disagree about API design, naming, scope, and priorities. State your view, listen to the counter-arguments, and be willing to update. "You raised a good point — I changed my mind" is one of the most respected things you can say in open source.

- **Make it easy to say yes.** Small, focused PRs. Clear commit messages. A test that demonstrates the change. Documentation updated alongside code. These are gifts to your reviewers and to your future self.

- **Help the next person.** If you figured out something that wasn't documented, write it down. If a tricky issue is fixed, leave a note in the changelog or a comment in the code explaining the trick. The goal is to make the path easier for whoever comes next.

- **Have fun.** This is a side project for many of us. Banter, jokes, and the occasional ASCII art are welcome. Joy is a feature, not a bug.

## When things get hard

Even great communities hit rough patches — a heated thread, a misread tone, a frustrating bug at the end of a long day. When that happens:

- Take a breath before replying.
- Default to the most generous interpretation of the other person's words.
- It's always okay to step away from a thread for a day.
- A simple "sorry, I came across more sharply than I meant to" goes a long way.

If something feels off and you'd like to talk to a maintainer about it privately, reach out via the contact listed in the project README, or open a private channel via GitHub. We'd rather hear about a small thing early than a big thing later.
